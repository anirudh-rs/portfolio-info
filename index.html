01
Data Science · ML · Network Analysis · Geospatial
The Collapse Signature Project

A full end-to-end data science pipeline investigating whether 4,999 years of civilisational collapse data reveals statistically significant patterns in timing, geography, causality, and resilience. Built on a hand-curated master dataset of 112 civilisations with 54 engineered features, drawing from CLIO-INFRA, Maddison Project, historical atlases, and primary scholarship. Delivered as a three-page Streamlit app — The Story (findings in plain English with statistical proof behind expandable sections), History in Motion (animated GIF territorial maps across 48 geospatial snapshots with an interactive boundary explorer), and The Proof (full clustering stats, spatial autocorrelation, network graph, SHAP chart, counterfactual engine, and complete dataset browser).

Five findings confirmed by permutation testing: collapses cluster in time (Z=6.65, p<0.0001) but Moran's I is negative across all five clusters — geographically dispersed, shared systemic cause. 67% align with climate or pandemic events, yet more stressors correlates with slower collapse (r=+0.41). Logistic regression achieves 92.0% leave-one-out accuracy — rise duration is the dominant predictor (SHAP=2.05). Longest causal chain: 7 civilisations, 1,884 years unbroken. Counterfactual engine: Qin Dynasty with Han's institutional patience +81.8% survival; Napoleon with Byzantine resilience +93.0%. Network analysis surfaces Britain as highest out-degree predator and Rome as highest betweenness centrality — both validated against historical consensus without encoding them explicitly.

Global Earthquake Risk & Seismic Activity Dashboard

A real-time seismic risk intelligence platform with no pre-downloaded datasets — every analysis is built fresh from a live USGS Earthquake Catalog API call. The pipeline parses nested GeoJSON into a structured DataFrame, enriches each event with reverse-geocoded country and continent attribution, USGS significance score, tsunami flags, felt reports, and CDI/MMI shaking intensity. K-Means clustering identifies the top 8 global earthquake hotzones from raw coordinates with no geographic prior, overlaid on a Folium world map alongside peer-reviewed PB2002 tectonic plate boundaries. A transparent 0–100 composite risk score per country blends event count, peak magnitude, USGS significance, and shallow event percentage, with a rules-based Notable Events narrative generating plain-English bullet points grounded in verifiable data checks — no LLM, no hallucination risk.

Greece recorded 291% above its 90-day baseline during the April 2026 test period — flagged automatically. A Nevada M4.78 event generated 764 public felt reports despite modest magnitude, correctly surfaced as the most widely-felt event via shallow depth scoring. K-Means independently identified the Ring of Fire's major subduction zones matching known seismological boundaries with no geographic input. The Turkey-Syria February 2023 window surfaces a textbook aftershock signature: M7.8 spike on February 6 followed by exponential decay with 570+ aftershocks in 24 hours visible in the daily activity chart. Statistical guardrails suppress misleading deviations when baseline sample sizes are too small — the dashboard is honest about when its own outputs can't be trusted.

03
SQL · PostgreSQL · Data Engineering
Sales Funnel Drop-off Analysis

A full end-to-end SQL analytics pipeline built entirely in PostgreSQL 18.3 — no Python, no external libraries — processing 109 million rows of real e-commerce events from an Eastern European electronics retailer across October and November 2019. Covers every stage a production data analyst would own: staging table ingestion of 14.2GB raw CSVs, a 5-script preprocessing pipeline (null audit, deduplication via ROW_NUMBER with ctid, DST-aware timestamp normalisation, IQR outlier detection, and a master clean view), followed by 5 analysis scripts covering funnel conversion, drop-off by brand and category, week-over-week trends using LAG/LEAD, and revenue concentration using NTILE and PERCENTILE_CONT. Visualised across 3 Tableau Public dashboards and a standalone HTML/CSS/Chart.js dashboard.

Overall funnel conversion 12.90%. The view-to-cart stage leaks 80.69% of users — 4,095,942 people lost at a single step. Total revenue $315M across 9 weeks. Singles Day drove 21.53% of 9-week revenue in one week with cart abandonment spiking to 58.51% — users loaded carts and deferred to Black Friday. The most surprising finding: cart abandonment is not a price problem. Converters and abandoners had virtually identical average cart prices ($209.70 vs $208.06, a $1.64 gap). The real differentiator was engagement depth — converted users viewed 40% more products and had twice as many cart interactions. Discounting is the wrong lever; product discovery is the right one. Top 10% of buyers generate 49.83% of revenue — a 50/10 distribution more concentrated than the classic Pareto rule.

04
ML · Clustering · Retail Analytics
Retail Customer Segmentation & Targeting Tool

A full end-to-end customer analytics pipeline processing 62 million transactions across three public retail datasets — UCI Online Retail II (UK gift retailer), H&M Fashion, and Instacart Grocery — unified into a single 7-column schema. Each dataset required different cleaning logic: UCI had 243k anonymous transactions and 18k cancellations, H&M had 2.97 million duplicates, and Instacart had no prices or dates requiring proxy engineering. An RFM scoring engine using quintile-based 1–5 scoring with inverted recency ranking segments 1,574,368 customers. K-Means with k=4 (deliberately overriding the silhouette-optimal k=2 in favour of actionable business output) identifies VIP, Champions, Regulars, and Lapsed tiers. Deployed as a six-page Streamlit dashboard with a segment explorer, marketing targeting tool, and churn risk report.

The same four-tier cluster structure emerged independently from three completely different retailers with no shared parameters — suggesting the RFM four-tier model reflects genuine retail customer behaviour, not an artefact of any one dataset. H&M VIP tier: 64,204 customers averaging 33 orders and £353 lifetime spend. Instacart Power Shoppers: 11,762 customers averaging 62 orders and 731 items lifetime. UCI outlier impact: 59 wholesale accounts (1% of customers) distorted all four clusters before 99th percentile capping. Scale: 62,027,333 transactions across 1,574,368 unique customers.

A/B Test Results Analysis

A complete statistical experimentation framework built entirely in PostgreSQL — no Python or R for analysis. A 4-table relational schema stores 882,000+ rows from two real e-commerce experiments. The statistical engine computes z-scores, p-values via a SQL implementation of the normal CDF using the error function, 95% confidence intervals, and pooled proportion z-tests — all in pure SQL, mirroring how experimentation analytics works inside data teams at scale. Three independent validation methods (z-scores, p-values, confidence intervals) all consistent with each other. Results written back into a persistent analysis_summary table. Three Tableau Public dashboards cover experiment results, ad exposure deep dive, and test integrity.

Landing page test returned no significant difference (z=1.24, p=0.39) — do not ship, despite 294,000 users in test. Ad group drove 43% relative conversion uplift over PSA (z=7.37, p≈0.00). Users shown 100+ ads converted at 17.14% vs 0.33% for 1-10 ad exposures — a 52x difference. Traffic split held at a perfect 50/50 every day across all 23 days. 1.3% of users received mismatched page assignments — a data quality issue caught and flagged. Conversion rates stabilised within 3 days confirming adequate test duration. The inconclusive landing page result is as valuable as the significant ad result — it prevented a wasted engineering rollout.

08
ML · Data Engineering · PostgreSQL · Tableau
US Flight Delay Prediction System

A fully reproducible end-to-end data science pipeline predicting whether a US domestic flight will be delayed and by how much. Ingests and cleans 3 million raw BTS flight records, loads them into PostgreSQL, engineers 23 predictive features through SQL aggregations and Python transformations, fetches real historical weather data from Open-Meteo API across 142 US airports (covering 95.8% of flight volume), trains two XGBoost models, and exports purpose-built analytical CSVs into a four-dashboard Tableau Public publication. Pandemic years 2020–2021 deliberately excluded — structurally abnormal delay patterns would degrade model generalisation to normal operations. Switched from regression (R²=0.04) to four-bracket classification after diagnosing that exact minute prediction is fundamentally limited without real-time ATC data.

Binary delay classifier: ROC-AUC 0.7203, competitive with published academic benchmarks (0.68–0.75 range). Bracket classifier: Weighted F1 0.595, on-time precision 89% — when the model says on-time it's right nearly 9 times out of 10. Departure hour is the strongest predictor (SHAP=0.38), nearly double the second-ranked feature. Route delay rate (SHAP=0.23) reveals certain routes are structurally delayed regardless of conditions. Carrier reliability outranks weather as a predictor — who you fly with matters more than the forecast. Friday evening departures from major hubs consistently show highest delay rates across all three years.

10
AI · RAG · NLP · LangChain
PL 24/25 Assist — Premier League RAG Assistant

An end-to-end RAG pipeline enabling natural language Q&A over 21 Premier League Wikipedia documents. PDFs loaded via PyPDFLoader, chunked with RecursiveCharacterTextSplitter (chunk size 1000, overlap 200), embedded with OpenAI text-embedding-3-small, and stored in a persistent ChromaDB vector store. On each query: the pipeline expands the query with season and club context, retrieves k=14 chunks, reorders by source priority, injects standings chunks for position/comparison questions, labels each chunk by type, and passes labelled context with sliding window conversation history (last 3 turns) to GPT-4o via ChatPromptTemplate. Returns answers with page-level source citations. Club focus mode shifts both retrieval priority and system prompt framing without any architectural change.

3,097 total chunks across 21 documents. Basic RAG with k=10 and no query expansion returned completely irrelevant chunks — wrong clubs in 100% of retrieved results. Query expansion alone fixed club-specific retrieval. Standings injection was necessary for comparison questions — without it GPT-4o cited Europa League qualification to rank teams rather than reading the actual table. Context labelling eliminated cross-contamination hallucinations. Conversation memory enables accurate follow-up resolution — "how many points did they finish with?" correctly resolves "they" from prior turn. ChromaDB committed to repo to avoid cold-start re-ingestion on Streamlit Cloud's ephemeral filesystem.

AI · Audio · ML · Streamlit
AI Stem Separation & Remix Tool

A full audio intelligence pipeline: file upload → AI stem separation via Meta Demucs htdemucs_6s → feature extraction via librosa → genre classification via per-stem XGBoost models → visualisation dashboard → PDF/CSV report generation → cloud deployment. Per-stem XGBoost models rather than one generic classifier — drums use zero crossing rate and beat count, piano and guitar use chroma and harmonic content, vocals use MFCCs. Confidence thresholding at 55%: predictions below this return Inconclusive rather than a wrong answer — a deliberate acknowledgement that GTZAN was trained on full mixed songs, not isolated stems. Two songs can be analysed independently in parallel without overwriting each other, with a comparison tab.

67% genre classification accuracy across 10 genres on 1,000 GTZAN samples — versus 10% random baseline. Classical achieved 100% accuracy — most distinct sonic fingerprint of all genres. Rock scored lowest at 40% — most confused with metal and country. Bohemian Rhapsody and Don't Stop Me Now both demonstrated guitar/piano frequency overlap limitation (300–2000Hz shared range). Blackbird and Someone Like You separated correctly with zero false detections — sparse acoustic arrangements work best. Model trained automatically on first run via Hugging Face — models too large for the repo are detected on startup and trained from the CSV already in the repository.
